What do I sound like online? - GMail vs. Facebook.¶

A machine-learning driven exploration of online speech patterns¶

In this project, I want to explore several questions about my online speech patterns, as well as the speech patterns of other people. I'll be asking five main questions:

  1. What words do I use that differentiate myself the greatest from my friends over Facebook Messenger?
  2. Which people do I talk to on Facebook Messenger - and how frequently do I talk to each of them? Do I talk to some significantly more than others?
  3. Do I use different words to talk to different people on Facebook?
  4. How do I express myself differently via e-mail as opposed to via Facebook Messenger?
  5. Do the emails I send contain more words on average than the messages I send on Facebook?

To answer these questions, I'll be using two primary source of data:

  1. Facebook messenger data
  2. GMail message data

In this notebook, I'll be exploring the differences between my email and Facebook messenger data. I'll attempt to answer the last two questions:

  1. How do I express myself differently via e-mail as opposed to via Facebook Messenger?
  2. Do the emails I send contain more words on average than the messages I send on Facebook?

Let's find the accuracy rate of this model.

In [11]:
test_vector = vectorizer.transform(test_text)
accuracy = 0
for i, actual_label in enumerate(test_labels):
    case = test_vector[i]
    predicted_label = classifier.predict(case[0])[0]
    if predicted_label == actual_label:
        accuracy += 1

print("{} out of {} correct, with an accuracy rate of {}%".format(
    accuracy, len(test_labels), round(100*accuracy/len(test_labels))
))

4. Conclusion¶

It's interesting to me that one of the primary words that differentiates my email messages from my facebook messages is the use of my full name ("Angelina Li") - I think this is reflective of one of the biggest perceptive differences between email and messages - email fells like a much more formal medium.

It's unsurprising that my email messages and facebook messages differ in length on average! Again, because it's a more formal medium, email messages often contain more information than facebook messages.
