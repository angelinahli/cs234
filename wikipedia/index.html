Wikipedia Project - Summary

Introduction

In this project, we studied knowledge production and bias in Wikipedia by analyzing edits in Hillary Rodham Clinton's Wikipedia page. Here is the project blog.

As previous scholars have noted, the controversiality of a given Wikipedia page will fluctuate as its editors and subject evolve over time. In this project, we study how Wikipedia pages become more or less controversial over time. In particular, we examine the phenomena of edit wars on Wikipedia pages. According to Wikipedia itself, edit wars occur "when editors who disagree about the content of a page repeatedly override each other's contributions". We chose to examine Hillary Clinton's Wikipedia page as a case study in edit wars.

Our research question was: Did big news stories associated with Hillary Clinton cause more edit wars to occur on her page? We attempt to answer this question by looking at the controversiality of Clinton's page just before and right after controversial events occurred in her career.

Our initial hypothesis is that big news stories about Hillary Clinton caused more edit wars to occur on her page.

Methodology

To measure the controversiality of Hillary Clinton's page, we created a controversiality index equalling the number of reversions on a page as a proportion of the total number of edits on that page, over a set period of time. We first measured the controversiality of Clinton's page on a monthly basis, then on a weekly basis.

We chose to look at 4 significant events in Hillary's career:

  1. Clinton takes responsibility for casualties of U.S. diplomats in Benghazi, Libya - October 15, 2012
  2. Clinton declares her intention to run for President of the United States - April 12, 2015
  3. Media starts reporting on James Comey investigation into Clinton's emails - May 5, 2016
  4. 2016 Election Night - November 8, 2016

We chose to look at these particular events because they occured after the creation of Wikipedia, occured on a set date (as opposed to occuring over the course of several months or years), and each have their own subsection on Hillary's page.

We first surveyed the data overall, looking at the general trend in how controversial HRC’s page was over time, and then we looked at how the controversiality of her page changed after each of these significant events.

Analysis

Overall, it seems like there was some change in how controversial a page was after a significant event in HRC’s career - in particular it’s promising that there was a big change in how controversial her page was after the James Comey incident -- this is because while there might have been a lot of anticipation of the election night prior to its occurrence, the public didn’t know about the Comey incident before it was released to the press, and thus it’s more likely that the significant news story caused the rise in controversiality in the page (because it was unanticipated).
