Food Observations Project

Introduction

From September 5-18 2017, I took photos of all the meals I ate. This page summarizes the results from the experiment.

This project was an attempt to investigate what I eat on a day to day basis, using photos as a data collection medium. My hope was that, if I could compile a fairly accurate and representative snapshot of what I generally eat, I would be able to answer some basic survey questions about my diet, such as: How much food do I waste when I eat? When do I typically start eating? How long do I usually eat for? How many of my meals contain vegetables or fruits?

There are unfortunately some confounding factors that might affect the interpretability of my results. Over the course of this experiment, I felt some mental pressure to try and waste less, eat healthier and eat more consistently than I usually do, with the knowledge my performance would be assessed later on. Furthermore, there were a few meals I forgot to take photos of. Unfortunately, I suspect these meals were correlated with my trips off campus and times I ate slightly unhealthier food (i.e. I didnâ€™t randomly forget to record observations). Nevertheless, I still expect my findings to at least broadly uncover my eating habits during the school year.

This page was created as part of Wellesley College's CS234 class.

Data Collection

At the end of the experiment, I had collected 36 unique food observations over the span of 14 days, averaging 2.57 observations per day. Most of my food observations were collected by taking photos of my food, and then analyzing that data afterwards using Python scripts and by eye to note the attributes of each meal.

Conclusion

The result that surprised me the most from this experiment so far was the amount of food I waste. I was honestly expecting to find that I waste more food than I did during this experiment, so I'm gratified that I'm not as bad of a food waster as I think. Nonetheless, throwing away approximately 5 plates of food over the course of two weeks is definitely not an achievement, and is something I want to tackle in my life moving forwards.
